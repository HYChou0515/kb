Particle contamination during plasma via etch is a recognized cause of
post-CMP metal shorts at the next metal layer. Common particle sources:
chamber wall flaking after extended use, polymer accumulation from fluorocarbon
chemistries, and erosion of the focus ring or electrode. Particles deposited at
the bottom of partially-etched vias or on the field oxide surface lead to
residual conductive material after barrier deposition and Cu plating, which
survives CMP and bridges adjacent metal lines as M2 shorts.
